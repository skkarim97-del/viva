Redesign the Today screen to be significantly cleaner, more minimal, and more premium by reducing visible elements and using progressive disclosure.

## Core principle

The screen should:

* show fewer things at once
* prioritize output over input
* reduce visual clutter
* feel calm and intentional

---

## Remove stacked inputs

Currently, the screen shows:

* feeling
* energy
* stress
* hydration
* life load
* training

All at once.

This must be changed.

---

## New interaction model

### Default view (collapsed)

Show ONLY:

1. Daily State
2. Headline
3. One-line drivers
4. Feeling input (single row)
5. Your Day plan

Do NOT show:

* energy
* stress
* hydration
* life load
* training

by default.

---

## Add “Refine” interaction

Below feeling input, add:

“Refine your day”

When tapped:

* expand a hidden section
* reveal additional inputs

---

## Expanded section (progressive disclosure)

Show:

* Energy
* Stress
* Hydration
* Life Load
* Training Intent

Each should:

* be in clean grouped rows
* have more spacing
* feel secondary

---

## Visual grouping

Group inputs like this:

### Primary

“How are you feeling?”

### Secondary (hidden by default)

Refine your day:

* Energy
* Stress
* Hydration
* Life Load
* Training

---

## Reduce text

Remove:

* bullet lists under headline
* duplicate explanation text
* long descriptive sentences

Replace with:

* short phrases
* one-line summaries

Example:
“Sleep solid · Recovery moderate · Energy high”

---

## Typography improvements

* Increase size of headline
* Reduce size of supporting text
* Use fewer text styles overall
* Increase spacing between sections

---

## Button / chip styling

* reduce size slightly
* increase spacing between chips
* use softer backgrounds
* avoid heavy outlines
* selected state should be subtle, not bright

---

## Color usage

* limit to 1–2 accent colors
* use blue or green only for selection
* avoid mixing too many colors in one view

---

## Card simplification

“Your Day” card:

* increase padding
* reduce text
* simplify wording

Example:
“50 min strength · full body”

instead of long descriptions.

---

## Spacing

* increase vertical spacing between sections
* avoid stacking elements tightly
* give each section breathing room

---

## Remove visual noise

* remove unnecessary dividers
* reduce icon usage
* avoid multiple emphasis styles

---

## Final goal

The screen should feel:

* minimal
* premium
* calm
* focused

The user should:

* understand their day instantly
* not feel overwhelmed
* not see too many decisions at once
