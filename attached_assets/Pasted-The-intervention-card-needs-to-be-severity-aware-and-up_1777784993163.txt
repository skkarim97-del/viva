The intervention card needs to be severity-aware and update live when symptoms improve or worsen.

Current issue:
If I change symptoms from negative to good/normal, the card still shows the same intervention-style recommendation. That is not right.

Required behavior:
The card should adapt based on symptom severity.

If symptoms are severe or worsening:
- Prioritize the most urgent symptom
- Give a stronger next-best action
- Make “Ask my care team” more visible if appropriate
- Mention if symptoms are heavier today

If symptoms are moderate:
- Give practical support and check back later
- Keep care team option available but not dominant

If symptoms are mild:
- Give lighter support
- Focus on preventing symptoms from getting worse
- Use softer copy like “stay ahead of it today”

If symptoms are good/normal:
- Do not show a heavy intervention
- Show a positive maintenance card or no card
- Suggested copy:
  “Your inputs look steady today. Keep following your plan and check in again if anything changes.”
- The plan can focus on consistency, hydration, protein or routine, not symptom rescue

Examples:

1. Severe nausea + low appetite:
Title:
Settle nausea first

Copy:
Start with small sips of water and a few bites of a bland protein-forward food. If nausea feels hard to manage or gets worse, ask your care team to review.

2. Moderate nausea + low appetite:
Title:
Settle nausea without skipping nutrition

Copy:
Try a small bland snack and sip water slowly for 20 to 30 minutes.

3. Mild nausea:
Title:
Stay ahead of nausea

Copy:
Keep meals smaller today and sip water steadily.

4. Great energy + strong appetite + no nausea + fine digestion + bowel movement yes:
Title:
Stay steady today

Copy:
Your check-in looks steady. Keep following your plan and check in again if anything changes.

Important:
- Live symptom changes should update the same card within 1-2 seconds
- Better symptoms should reduce the intensity of the recommendation
- Worse symptoms should increase urgency and care-team prominence
- Do not show the same recommendation regardless of severity
- Do not create duplicate cards
- Use the existing HIPAA/OpenAI guardrails
- If no meaningful symptoms are present, either show a light maintenance card or hide the intervention card, whichever fits the current product flow better
