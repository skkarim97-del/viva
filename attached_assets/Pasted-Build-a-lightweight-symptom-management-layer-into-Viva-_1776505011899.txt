Build a lightweight symptom-management layer into Viva that connects common GLP-1 side effects to behavioral / lifestyle drivers and escalates to the doctor when symptoms appear persistent, worsening, or clinically concerning.

Goal

Viva should not just log symptoms. It should:

1. identify likely behavioral contributors
2. provide simple patient self-management guidance when appropriate
3. flag the doctor when symptoms are persistent, severe, or worsening

Symptoms to support first

* nausea
* constipation
* low appetite

Behavioral / lifestyle inputs to connect

Add or use simple daily inputs for:

* hydration
* protein intake
* movement
* fiber intake if easy
* bowel movement yes/no if easy
* dose taken
* recent dose increase if available

Patient-side logic

For MVP, use lightweight rules-based logic.

Examples:

* nausea + low hydration or long gap without eating → suggest hydration, smaller meals, bland foods, slower eating
* constipation + low water + low movement → suggest hydration, walking, gradual fiber increase
* low appetite + low protein intake → suggest prioritizing protein and smaller nutrient-dense meals

Escalation logic

If symptoms are:

* severe
* persistent across multiple days
* worsening
* or occurring after dose increase

then do not just show self-management guidance. Also flag the doctor in the dashboard.

Doctor dashboard output

For flagged patients, show:

* symptom issue
* persistence / severity
* likely contributing behavioral factors
* whether patient has already received basic self-management guidance
* suggested follow-up indicator

Patient UX

Keep guidance:

* short
* supportive
* practical
* non-alarmist
* not overly medical

Guardrails

Do not position this as diagnosis or treatment.
Frame it as common symptom-management support and escalate when symptoms look concerning.

Constraints

* keep this rules-based for MVP
* no complex AI system yet
* no overengineering
* start with nausea, constipation, and low appetite only

My take

This makes Viva much better because now it becomes:

* a retention tool
* a symptom support layer
* a triage layer

That is much more compelling than just “we detect churn.”

The only rule is: keep it narrow and safe.

The best version of MVP is not “solve every symptom.”
It’s:

* help with 3 common issues
* escalate intelligently
* keep patients on treatment longer
