Design and implement a premium onboarding flow that makes PulsePilot feel highly personalized, intelligent, and simple.

## Core Goal

Onboarding should:

* feel fast and intuitive
* require minimal typing
* use mostly taps and selections
* establish a strong sense of personalization immediately
* configure the AI coaching system with enough context to generate meaningful recommendations on day one

The user should feel:
“This app understands my goals and situation already.”

---

## Step-by-step onboarding flow

### 1. Welcome screen

* Clean, minimal design
* Short value proposition:
  “Your daily health and performance coach”
* Subtext:
  “Personalized guidance for fitness, recovery, sleep, and mental well-being”
* Primary CTA: “Get Started”

---

### 2. Goal selection (multi-select)

Ask:
“What do you want to improve?”

Options:

* Lose weight
* Build muscle
* Improve fitness
* Increase energy
* Sleep better
* Reduce stress
* Stay consistent
* General health

Allow multiple selections.

---

### 3. Basic profile (quick inputs)

Keep UI extremely simple.

Inputs:

* Age (optional)
* Sex (optional)
* Height
* Current weight
* Goal weight (if weight loss or gain selected)

Use:

* sliders
* steppers
* tap-based input

---

### 4. Activity level

Ask:
“How active are you right now?”

Options:

* Mostly inactive
* Lightly active (walking, occasional workouts)
* Moderately active (3–4 workouts/week)
* Very active (5+ workouts/week)

---

### 5. Training preference

Ask:
“How much time can you train?”

Options:

* Under 30 min/day
* 30–60 min/day
* 60–90 min/day
* 90+ min/day

---

### 6. Mental & energy baseline

Ask:
“How have you been feeling recently?”

Options:

* Consistently energized
* Generally good
* Often tired
* Frequently stressed
* Burnt out

Keep this simple and non-clinical.

---

### 7. Sleep habits

Ask:
“What does your sleep look like?”

Options:

* 7–8 hours consistently
* 6–7 hours
* Under 6 hours
* Inconsistent schedule

Then optionally:
“What time do you usually go to bed?”
“What time do you wake up?”

(Keep optional or quick-select)

---

### 8. Device integration

Prompt user to connect:

* Apple Health (HealthKit)
* Garmin
* WHOOP

Explain clearly:
“This helps personalize your daily recommendations automatically.”

Also include:
“Skip for now” option

---

### 9. Personalization summary (important)

Before entering the app, show:

“You’re set up”

Then show a short summary:

* Goal: Lose weight and improve energy
* Plan: Moderate training, consistent activity
* Focus: Build sustainable habits

CTA:
“Start your plan”

---

## Backend logic (critical)

Use onboarding inputs to calculate:

### 1. Caloric needs

* Estimate maintenance calories (TDEE)
* Apply a reasonable deficit or surplus based on goal
* Keep recommendations moderate and sustainable

Example:

* Weight loss: 300–500 calorie deficit
* Muscle gain: slight surplus with protein emphasis

### 2. Protein targets

* Based on body weight and goal

### 3. Activity targets

* Steps
* Workout frequency
* Training intensity

### 4. Sleep targets

* Based on current sleep vs ideal
* Identify sleep debt and consistency issues

### 5. Recovery baseline

* Initialize expected recovery patterns until wearable data improves accuracy

---

## Product behavior

The system should:

* combine onboarding inputs + wearable data
* continuously update recommendations
* adapt plans over time

If wearable data exists:

* prioritize real data

If not:

* rely on onboarding assumptions

---

## UI design requirements (critical)

The onboarding must feel:

* premium
* smooth
* minimal
* fast
* visually refined

Design direction:

* full-screen steps
* large typography
* clean spacing
* subtle animations between steps
* soft transitions
* minimal text per screen

Each screen should:

* ask one question only
* use large tap targets
* avoid clutter
* feel effortless

---

## Visual inspiration

Use inspiration from:

* Apple onboarding flows
* Headspace
* Calm
* Oura
* WHOOP
* top-performing App Store apps

But ensure the final design:

* feels unique
* not generic
* more minimal and premium

---

## Micro-interactions

Add:

* smooth transitions between steps
* subtle haptic feedback on selections
* progress indicator (top bar or dots)
* animated confirmation on completion

---

## Tone and copy

* simple
* calm
* confident
* no jargon
* no pressure
* no hype

Example tone:
“Let’s understand your baseline”
“Set your starting point”
“We’ll adapt this over time”

---

## Final goal

After onboarding, the user should open the app and see:

* a personalized daily plan
* tailored to their goals, baseline, and habits
* even before full wearable data is available

The onboarding should feel like:

* a quick setup
* not a questionnaire

And the result should feel:

* highly personalized
* immediately useful
