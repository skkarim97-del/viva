Upgrade Viva to include simple, actionable recommendations with built-in accountability tracking.

## Core goal

Transform the app from:

* passive recommendations

into:

* actionable daily plan
* lightweight habit tracking system

The experience must remain:

* simple
* clean
* not overwhelming

---

## Update “Your Day”

Each recommendation must be:

* specific
* measurable
* actionable
* short (one line)

Examples:

* Move: 30–45 min walk or workout
* Hydrate: drink 2–3L water
* Mind: 10 min meditation or quiet time
* Recover: in bed by 10:30pm
* Fuel: eat balanced meals, prioritize protein

---

## Add completion tracking

Each item should have:

* a checkbox or tap-to-complete interaction

Behavior:

* user taps → item becomes completed
* apply subtle green highlight
* smooth animation

---

## Limit number of actions

* show only 4–5 actions per day
* do not overload the user

---

## Track completion over time

Store:

* daily completion rate
* weekly consistency

Display:

* simple consistency score

Example:
“Consistency: 80% this week”

Keep this subtle and not gamified.

---

## Update tone of recommendations

Shift from fitness-focused language to holistic health.

Avoid:

* “train”
* “workout-focused language only”

Use:

* movement
* recovery
* hydration
* mental well-being
* balance

---

## Update headline examples

Replace:
“Train today. Keep it steady.”

With:

* “Stay active. Keep it balanced.”
* “Focus on consistency today.”
* “Take care of your body and mind today.”

---

## Add Daily Focus

Add a short section:

“Daily Focus”

Example:
“Stay consistent across movement, hydration, and recovery”

---

## UX requirements

* interactions must be instant
* tapping tasks must feel satisfying
* no friction
* no forms

---

## Visual design

* completed tasks → subtle green
* incomplete → neutral
* no aggressive colors
* maintain clean aesthetic

---

## Final goal

The user should feel:

“I know exactly what to do today and I can track if I did it.”

Not:

“I’m reading advice I may or may not follow.”
