Build a clinical onboarding flow for doctors that enforces patient participation and positions Viva as a monitoring platform, not a consumer wellness app.

Goal

Ensure doctors understand that:

the platform depends on patient app usage
patients must be invited and activated
monitoring begins only after patient check-ins
Step 1: Account creation

Page with:

name
email
password
CTA: “Create account”

Use professional, clinical tone. No mention of AI or coaching.

Step 2: Patient onboarding (core step)
Page title:

“Set up your patient panel”

Subtitle:

“Patients must connect to Viva to enable monitoring”

Section 1:

Clinic name input (simple)

Section 2:

Patient input form:

name
email (required)
medication
dose

Allow adding multiple patients

Add helper text:

“Patients will receive an invite to download the Viva app and begin daily check-ins.”

On submit:
create patient record
generate invite link
mark patient as “pending activation”
Show confirmation:

“Invite sent”

Optional:

resend invite
copy link
Step 3: Dashboard behavior

If no patients:

show empty state:
“Add and invite patients to begin monitoring”

If patients exist but not activated:

show “Pending activation” status
do not show risk scores yet

If patient has activated:

show risk, trends, and monitoring data
Add top banner:

“Monitoring begins once patients complete their first check-in in the Viva app”

Design constraints
clean, minimal, professional UI
no consumer wellness language
no AI references
no demo-first experience
no complex setup flows
no unnecessary fields
My pushback (important)

This is the right move strategically.

You are:

increasing friction slightly upfront
BUT
massively increasing product integrity and real usage

If doctors can use this without patients:
→ it becomes a toy

If they need patients:
→ it becomes infrastructure

Bottom line

Your onboarding should communicate one thing clearly:

“This system works when your patients are active. Invite them first.”

That’s what makes Viva real.
