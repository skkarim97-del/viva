Refactor Viva to focus on a small set of core health metrics and a proprietary interpretation layer.

## Core Product Philosophy

Viva should not rely on dozens of integrations or metrics.

Instead:

* use a small set of core inputs
* interpret them intelligently
* produce simple, actionable outputs

The goal is:
Few inputs → strong interpretation → clear daily guidance

---

## Core Inputs

Use only the following inputs for MVP:

### Objective data (from device or manual entry)

* sleep (duration and consistency)
* steps / activity
* heart rate (resting and trend)
* HRV (if available)
* calories burned (optional)

### Subjective data (user input)

* energy level
* stress level
* mood / overall feeling

These inputs must be sufficient to generate all recommendations.

---

## Do NOT rely on third-party scores

Do not use:

* Garmin Body Battery
* Apple readiness scores
* WHOOP strain scores

Instead:

* create a proprietary interpretation layer

---

## Create internal signals

Normalize raw inputs into simple internal scores:

* sleep score
* activity score
* recovery score (HRV + heart rate)
* stress score (user input + signals)
* consistency score

These scores should NOT be directly shown to the user.

They are used internally.

---

## Create Daily State

Use internal signals to assign a Daily State:

* Recover
* Maintain
* Build
* Push

This is the primary output shown to users.

---

## Create Key Drivers

Identify the top 2–3 reasons driving the Daily State.

Examples:

* Sleep is strong
* Activity has been consistent
* Recovery is slightly lower than usual
* Stress is elevated

These must be simple and easy to understand.

---

## AI as interpretation layer

Use the ChatGPT API as the central intelligence layer.

Provide the model with:

* normalized signals
* user goals
* recent trends
* user inputs (energy, stress, mood)

The model should generate:

1. Daily headline
2. Daily focus
3. Your Day plan:

   * Move
   * Fuel
   * Recover
   * Mind
4. Why explanation

---

## AI response rules

Responses must be:

* short
* clear
* actionable
* based on actual user data

Avoid:

* generic advice
* long paragraphs
* technical jargon

---

## Example output

Daily State:
Maintain

Headline:
Stay consistent today

Your Day:

* Move: 30–45 min moderate activity
* Fuel: Eat balanced meals, prioritize protein
* Recover: Wind down earlier tonight
* Mind: Keep stress low and avoid overstimulation

Why:

* Sleep was solid
* Activity has been consistent
* Recovery is slightly below peak

---

## Manual-first support

If no wearable is connected:

* allow manual input for sleep, activity, mood, stress
* still generate meaningful recommendations

The app must work fully without a wearable.

---

## Product goal

Viva should feel like:

* a simple daily health coach
* not a complex data tracker
* not dependent on external scoring systems

The user should feel:
“I understand what to do today based on my body and how I feel.”
