Please do a final 10/10 pilot readiness pass for the Viva Care mobile app, focused on making the core thesis unmistakable:

Behavioral signals + Apple Health/wearables + dose context → personalized symptom support → adaptive plan → patient feedback → provider escalation.

Do not add random features.
Do not bring back the standalone AI coach.
Do not redesign the whole app.
Tighten the existing Today, Week and Trends flows so they feel premium, cohesive and pilot-ready.

The goal is to make the app clearly support the Viva deck thesis:
- real-time visibility between visits
- personalized micro-interventions
- provider escalation with context
- better engagement and retention signal capture

==================================================
1. TODAY TAB: MAKE SYMPTOM SUPPORT THE HERO
==================================================

The Symptom support card is the core feature. It should feel like the product’s intelligence layer, not a generic tip card.

Make sure the card clearly communicates:

1. What Viva noticed
2. Why it matters
3. What the patient should do now
4. How the patient can give feedback
5. When care-team review is appropriate

Add a compact signal/context line or chip row showing what informed the support.

Examples:
- Today’s check-in
- Recent symptoms
- Around dose timing
- Apple Health trends
- Activity lower than usual
- Sleep lower than usual
- Prior feedback

Rules:
- Only show Apple Health if connected and real data exists
- Only show dose timing if real dose context exists
- Do not show raw debug text
- Do not overexplain
- Keep chips short and readable

Suggested card hierarchy:

Badge:
Support today / Extra support / Preventive support / Review may help

Title:
Symptom support

Subtitle:
Personalized support based on today’s check-in

If recent patterns are used:
Personalized support based on today’s check-in and recent patterns

If Apple Health is used:
Personalized support based on today’s check-in and Apple Health trends

Primary recommendation:
Short action title
1-2 sentence body max
“Why this helps” in one short sentence

Buttons:
I’ll try this
Another option

Feedback after patient tries:
Better
Same
Worse

Care-team CTA:
Ask my care team to review

==================================================
2. MAKE ALL CARD STATES DISTINCT
==================================================

Stable day:
- green/blue calm accents
- no care-team CTA
- preventive support
- copy should feel like staying ahead, not another status card

Mild symptoms:
- “Stay ahead” tone
- light support
- no alarming language

Moderate symptoms:
- “Support today”
- practical symptom management
- recommendation should be specific and actionable

Heavier symptoms:
- amber/orange accents
- stronger recommendation
- care-team review visible if appropriate

Urgent/red flag:
- red only for true urgent or severe safety states
- do not use red for normal GLP-1 side effects unless highest severity or red-flag

Acceptance:
A patient should immediately understand whether today is stable, mild, moderate, heavier, or review-worthy.

==================================================
3. BUTTON FUNCTIONALITY QA
==================================================

Please verify and fix all Symptom support interactions.

I’ll try this:
- marks recommendation accepted
- persists after reload
- reveals feedback flow if intended
- does not create duplicate cards

Another option:
- changes the recommendation copy
- does not repeat the same recommendation
- works across nausea, appetite, digestion, energy and hydration states
- hides or disables gracefully if no alternative exists
- does not create duplicate cards

Better / Same / Worse:
- persists feedback
- updates UI confirmation
- “Worse” should increase prominence of care-team review
- no broken repeated submissions

Ask my care team to review:
- works even if patient has not tapped “I’ll try this”
- pre-accepts the intervention if required
- avoids duplicate escalations from repeated taps
- shows patient-facing success state
- creates useful provider-side context

Success copy:
Review requested. Your care team can see today’s symptoms and support history.

Error copy:
Couldn’t request review. Please try again.

No visible debug text should appear in the patient UI.

==================================================
4. PROVIDER ESCALATION CONTEXT
==================================================

When a patient requests review or reports Worse, the provider dashboard should receive a structured escalation summary.

Include:
- current symptom selections
- severity level
- recent pattern if available
- dose timing context if available
- Apple Health context if available
- intervention shown
- whether patient tapped “I’ll try this”
- feedback result if available
- timestamp
- suggested review reason

Example provider summary:
Patient reported moderate nausea, low appetite and constipation. Symptoms appear around dose timing. Viva recommended slow hydration and bland foods. Patient requested review.

Do not include PHI in any OpenAI payload.
Do not send names, DOBs, emails, clinic names, provider names or identifiers to OpenAI.
Do not make medication dose-change recommendations.

7. TRENDS TAB SHOULD PROVE INTELLIGENCE
==================================================

Trends should clearly show the intelligence layer.

It should surface:
- check-in patterns
- dose timing patterns
- intervention response patterns
- Apple Health/wearable trends when available
- consistency/engagement patterns

Examples:
- Appetite tends to dip 2 days after dose day
- Nausea improved after slow hydration support
- Energy was lower after lighter sleep
- Activity tends to dip on higher-symptom days
- Digestion symptoms tend to appear when appetite is lower

Rules:
- Do not overclaim causation
- Use “tends to,” “often,” “appears,” or “has shown up”
- Do not say Apple Health was used unless connected
- Keep each trend short and patient-friendly

Update subtitle logic:
If Apple Health connected:
Patterns from your check-ins and Apple Health

If not connected:
Patterns from your recent check-ins
