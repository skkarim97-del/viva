Upgrade the Insights section to deliver deeper, more valuable, and more differentiated insights based on the user’s overall health and behavior.

## Core goal

Insights should NOT just summarize data.

They should:

* interpret patterns
* connect multiple signals
* explain what it means
* suggest a clear implication or action

The user should feel:
“This actually understands what’s going on with me.”

---

## 1. Insight structure (required)

Each insight should follow:

1. Signal (what’s happening)
2. Meaning (what it indicates)
3. Implication (what to do or expect)

Keep it to 1–2 short lines max.

---

## 2. Data inputs to combine

Insights must combine multiple signals, not single metrics.

Use:

* HRV vs baseline
* sleep duration + sleep trend
* recent activity (last 3–5 days)
* energy input
* stress input
* hydration input
* consistency (task completion)

Do NOT generate insights from a single metric in isolation.

---

## 3. Example upgraded insights

Replace simple outputs like:
“HRV is up this week”

With:

* “Your recovery is improving, but your sleep has been inconsistent — push today, but prioritize a strong wind-down tonight.”
* “You’ve been active but under-recovered the last few days — a lighter session today will help you sustain progress.”
* “Energy is high and stress is low — this is a good window for a more demanding workout or focused work.”
* “Hydration and sleep are both trending down — this is likely driving lower energy today.”

---

## 4. Tone

Insights should feel:

* intelligent
* human
* practical
* slightly coaching-oriented

Avoid:

* robotic phrasing
* raw stats without meaning
* generic advice

---

## 5. Personalization

Insights must reflect:

* recent behavior patterns
* user inputs (feeling, stress, energy)
* what the user actually did vs was recommended

Examples:

* “You’ve skipped recovery routines this week — simplify it today instead of forcing it.”
* “You’ve been consistent with movement — maintain it rather than increasing intensity.”

---

## 6. Action bias

Each insight should imply a decision:

* push vs hold vs recover
* increase vs maintain vs reduce
* focus on sleep, hydration, or movement

Even if not explicitly labeled, the recommendation should be clear.

---

## 7. Output format

Keep insights:

* short (1–2 lines)
* scannable
* not paragraph-heavy

Example format:

“Your recovery is improving, but sleep has been inconsistent — keep intensity moderate and prioritize an earlier night.”

---

## 8. Avoid Garmin-style outputs

Do NOT:

* restate metrics
* focus on isolated trends
* give generic health tips

DO:

* connect signals
* explain cause + effect
* guide behavior

---

## 9. Final goal

Insights should feel like:

* a smart coach noticing patterns
* not a dashboard summarizing data

The user should think:

“This actually understands my body and my habits, and tells me what to do next.”
