Please redesign the Personalized check-in card to feel more premium, Apple-like and modern.

Goal:
Make the UX feel sleek, calm, high-tech and easy to scan. It should feel like a polished health-tech product, not a dense content card.

Design goals:
- cleaner
- more scannable
- more action-oriented
- less text-heavy
- more premium visual hierarchy

Please update the card with these principles:

1. Show one primary recommendation first
Instead of showing all recommendations equally, surface one main “next best step” first.

2. Reduce visible text
Shorten body copy and make each section easier to scan.

3. Use progressive disclosure
Show one main recommendation and collapse secondary recommendations into a “More support for today” section.

4. Improve hierarchy
The eye should immediately understand:
- what this is
- what the user should do first
- what other support is available

5. Improve CTA hierarchy
Use:
- primary: “I’ll try this”
- secondary: “Another option”
- tertiary/text action: “Ask my care team”

6. Make the UI less boxy
Use softer surfaces, fewer heavy borders, subtle shadows and better spacing.

7. Reduce reliance on all-caps labels
Use cleaner sentence-case labels where possible.

8. Add subtle motion
When the intervention appears or expands, use a soft fade/slide animation.

Suggested structure:

Today’s next steps
Based on your check-in, here’s what may help today.

What we noticed
You reported nausea and low energy today.

Primary recommendation tile
Settle nausea without skipping nutrition
Try 3 to 5 bites of yogurt, tofu, soup or a smoothie. Then sip water slowly for 20 to 30 minutes.

Buttons:
- I’ll try this
- Another option

More support for today
1 more step for appetite, energy and digestion

Expandable secondary recommendations below.

Footer:
If symptoms feel severe or urgent, contact your care team or seek medical help.

Important:
Keep it clean, sleek and very easy to read.
The card should feel like a premium guided action experience.
