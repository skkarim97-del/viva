Build a clean, minimal summary bar at the top of the doctor web dashboard, above the “Your patients” section.

Goal

Add a lightweight top summary that gives doctors an instant snapshot of their panel without cluttering the page or distracting from the patient queue. This should feel premium, modern, and very easy to scan.

Design direction

Keep it:

clean
minimal
horizontally aligned
visually consistent with the current Viva AI dashboard
subtle, not loud
premium and operational, not analytical-heavy

Do not add charts, graphs, spark lines, or dense tables.
Do not make it look like a corporate BI dashboard.
This should feel like a simple “panel health” header.

What to add

Create a top summary row with 4 small cards or pill-style stat blocks.

Use these 4 metrics:

Needs follow-up
count of patients currently in the “Needs follow-up” bucket
No check-in (3+ days)
count of patients whose last check-in was 3 or more days ago
Actions taken today
count of doctor notes created today
Total patients
total number of patients in the doctor’s panel
UX requirements
Place this summary directly under the page title area and above the grouped patient queue
It should be visible immediately when the doctor lands on the dashboard
Keep spacing generous and typography clean
Prioritize readability and elegance over data density
If possible, make each stat block subtly clickable:
“Needs follow-up” scrolls to that section
“No check-in (3+ days)” filters or scrolls to the relevant patients
“Actions taken today” can remain static if no action exists yet
“Total patients” can remain static
Visual guidance
Use the existing Viva AI palette and styling language
White or very light cards with soft borders or subtle shadows
Rounded corners
Large number, smaller label beneath or above it
Keep labels very short
Use subtle accent colors only where helpful:
red/orange tint for “Needs follow-up”
amber for “No check-in”
neutral/dark for “Actions taken today”
blue/neutral for “Total patients”
Important constraint

This should not overpower the queue.
The patient queue remains the main event.
The summary bar should feel like a calm, high-level snapshot, not the center of attention.
