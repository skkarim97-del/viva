Please make the following Viva Care onboarding and coach updates.

1. Start page logo alignment
- On the opening/start page, center the “Care” text directly under the viva logo.
- It should visually align with the logo lockup and not look offset.
- Keep the current styling otherwise unless minor spacing tweaks are needed.

2. Start page subtext copy
Replace the current subtext:
“A simple daily check-in plus Apple Health, so your care team sees how you're really doing between visits.”

With:
“Daily check-ins and Apple Health insights, so your care team can support you between visits.”

Keep line breaks responsive and visually balanced.

3. Coach response style rules
Update the coach/system prompt or response formatting rules so coach responses:
- Never use em dashes
- Use short, bite-sized responses
- Prefer 2 to 4 short sentences max
- Give 1 to 3 concrete next steps
- Avoid overly generic encouragement like “You’ve got this!” unless used very sparingly
- Avoid sounding like it is making clinical decisions
- Recommend contacting the care team when the user mentions stopping treatment, worsening symptoms, severe symptoms, uncertainty about medication, or concerns about side effects

4. Coach medication-stop detection
If a patient asks anything similar to:
- “Should I stop treatment?”
- “Should I stop my medication?”
- “Can I quit?”
- “Should I pause my dose?”
- “Should I skip my dose?”
- “I want to stop taking this”
Then:
- The coach should NOT advise them to stop or continue independently
- The response should say they should check with their care team before stopping or changing medication
- The app should create a care event / escalation event that appears in the clinician dashboard
- Classify this as a “Patient requested review” or “Treatment concern” flag, not necessarily an emergency unless emergency symptoms are also present

Suggested coach response for this case:
“Don’t stop or change your medication without checking with your care team.
If side effects are bothering you, your provider may adjust timing, dose, or supportive steps.
I can flag this for your care team to review.”

5. Safety distinction
Keep severe red-flag symptoms separate from normal treatment concerns.
Emergency symptoms should trigger stronger safety guidance.
Stopping-treatment questions should trigger clinician review because they are adherence and retention risk signals.

Please make these updates without changing the broader UI structure.
