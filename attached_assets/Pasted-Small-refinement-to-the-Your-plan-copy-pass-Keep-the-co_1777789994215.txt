Small refinement to the “Your plan” copy pass:

Keep the copy premium and concise, but do not make the recommendations too vague or purely qualitative.

I still want the plan items to be specific and actionable.

For MOVE, keep specific options like:
- Strength training
- Cardio
- Light movement
- Full rest day

But make the descriptions polished:
Strength training
20-30 minutes of light-to-moderate strength work, if energy feels steady.

Cardio
20-30 minutes of easy cardio. Keep the pace conversational.

Light movement
10-15 minutes of gentle movement, like walking or stretching.

Full rest day
Take pressure off today. Light movement is enough.

For FUEL, keep clear meal/protein guidance:
Gentle fueling
Keep meals small, simple, and protein-forward.

Protein support
Add protein to your next meal, like yogurt, tofu, soup, or a smoothie.

Small meals
Choose 2-3 smaller meals instead of forcing full portions.

Simple nutrition
A few bites or sips still count. Focus on what you can tolerate.

For HYDRATE, keep useful quantity ranges but make them less rigid:
Steady hydration
Aim for 6-8 cups across the day, adjusting for thirst and activity.

Slow hydration
Sip slowly over the next hour. Small amounts are easier when nausea shows up.

Hydration support
Keep fluids steady. Add electrolytes if they usually help you.

Fluids first
Start with small sips and build gradually.

For RECOVER, keep actual sleep targets where helpful:
Earlier wind-down
Begin winding down 30-60 minutes earlier tonight.

Sleep support
Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep, if your schedule allows.

Recovery focus
Keep today simple. Rest, fluids, and easy food are enough.

Consistent rest
Protect a steady bedtime and give your body room to recover.

Overall rule:
Each plan item should be:
- specific enough to act on
- concise enough to scan quickly
- premium enough to feel polished
- supportive without sounding generic

Avoid:
- vague lines like “support your body”
- overly broad advice with no action
- clunky titles like “Early wind-down + 8+ hours”
- harsh/scary claims like “Under-eating slows your metabolism”
- long explanations

Acceptance:
The “Your plan” section should still include specific recommendations, like strength training, cardio, light movement, full rest day, 6-8 cups hydration, and 7-8 hours sleep, but the wording should feel clean, premium, and easy to understand.
