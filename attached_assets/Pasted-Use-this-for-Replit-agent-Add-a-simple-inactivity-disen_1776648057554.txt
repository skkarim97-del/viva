---

Add a simple **inactivity / disengagement rule** to Viva, but do **not** treat inactivity as confirmed treatment stop.

## Goal

We need a clean MVP way to handle patients who disappear from the app without polluting retention analytics.

A patient who has not logged in or checked in for 12+ days should **not** automatically be marked as stopped treatment.

Instead, they should be treated as:

* **at_risk**
* **disengaged**
* or **unknown**
  depending on the current system design

Keep **confirmed stopped** separate from inactivity-based inference.

---

## Rule to implement

### Do NOT do this

* do not automatically mark a patient as `stopped` just because they have no login / no check-in for 12 days

### Do this instead

If a patient has:

* no app activity
* and/or no check-in
  for **12+ days**

then mark or surface them as:

* `at_risk`
* or `disengaged`
* or another lightweight non-final risk state

Use the cleanest existing status / risk model already in place.

If no explicit disengaged state exists yet, do not introduce a huge taxonomy. A simple derived flag is fine.

---

## What to build

### 1. Add a derived inactivity flag

Create a clean derived signal such as:

* `inactive_12d = true/false`
* or `disengaged = true/false`

Use:

* last app activity timestamp
* or last check-in timestamp
* whichever is already available and most reliable

Document exactly what source is used.

---

### 2. Surface it in analytics

In Viva Analytics, add a small metric in the relevant section:

* patients inactive 12+ days
* or patients flagged disengaged

This should be separate from:

* confirmed stopped
* unknown
* active

Do not merge these categories.

---

### 3. Surface it in Viva Clinic if easy

If easy, add a lightweight signal in the clinician dashboard or patient list:

* “Inactive 12+ days”
* or “Disengaged”

This should help clinicians know who may need outreach.

Do not redesign the workflow.

---

### 4. Keep confirmed churn clean

Only treat patients as `stopped` when:

* patient explicitly reports stopping
* doctor explicitly marks stopped

Do not let inactivity rewrite confirmed retention status.

---

## Optional extension if easy

If current logic supports it cleanly, allow the inactivity flag to contribute to:

* at-risk scoring
* escalation logic
* doctor review prioritization

Only if it fits naturally into the current system.

---

## Constraints

* keep this lightweight
* no new complex churn state machine
* no heavy dependencies
* no mobile / iOS / EAS changes unless absolutely required
* do not break current retention calculations
* do not conflate disengagement with confirmed churn

Keep it:
