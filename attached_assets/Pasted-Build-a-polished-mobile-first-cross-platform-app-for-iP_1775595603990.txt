Build a polished, mobile-first cross-platform app for iPhone and Android called PulsePilot.

Product concept

PulsePilot is a subscription-based AI health, fitness, recovery, and nutrition coach for everyday consumers. It connects to wearable and health data platforms like Apple Health, Garmin, and WHOOP, then translates that data into simple, science-backed daily guidance.

The product should feel easy enough for the average American to use every day, even if they know nothing about HRV, Zone 2, aerobic vs anaerobic training, recovery metrics, fasting protocols, macros, or training periodization.

All of the complexity should happen in the background. The user experience should be extremely simple, clear, and actionable.

Core promise

The app should help users answer:

What should I do today for my health?
Should I push hard, go easy, or recover?
What should I eat today based on my goals and activity?
Should I fast today or not?
Am I moving in the right direction?
What habits are helping or hurting my progress?

Core AI coaching engine

Build an AI engine that uses user data, goals, trends, and recent behavior to generate personalized wellness coaching.

The coaching engine should include:

Fitness guidance
Recovery guidance
Nutrition guidance
Fasting guidance
Habit formation guidance
Trend analysis
Readiness-based workout suggestions

The logic should account for:

HRV relative to baseline
Resting heart rate changes
Sleep quality and sleep debt
Recent training load
Step count and general activity
Weight and body composition trends
Goal type such as fat loss, muscle gain, general wellness, endurance, or consistency
Examples of coaching logic
If HRV is down, resting HR is elevated, and sleep is poor, recommend lower intensity, more recovery, hydration, and earlier bedtime
If recovery is strong and recent strain is low, recommend a harder workout
If the user is trying to lose weight and activity is low, increase movement targets and tighten calorie guidance
If the user is trying to gain muscle, increase protein targets and emphasize strength training progression
If the user is fasting but recovery is poor, suggest modifying the fasting window
If the user is accumulating fatigue, explain that simply and suggest a recovery day
If the user is doing too much high intensity work, recommend more Zone 2
If the user is undertraining for endurance, increase aerobic volume gradually
If the user is overdoing volume, reduce intensity or add rest
Scientific but simple communication style

The app should be deeply evidence-based in logic, but explain things in plain English.

Examples:
Do not say:

“Your autonomic nervous system markers indicate sympathetic dominance and insufficient parasympathetic recovery.”

Say:

“Your body looks more stressed than usual today. A lighter workout or recovery day is likely the better move.”

Do not overwhelm the user with technical jargon on the main screen. Keep advanced explanations secondary or optional.
